Reducing Wait Time in Theme Park Queue Lines

An Investigation into Queueing Theory, Suspended Reality, and Predicting Wait Times

Anya Kumar

Welcome to my capstone project! As the title suggests, I investigated the different factors that can help reduce the wait time for rides within theme parks.

Every year, over 75.33 million tourists visit Orlando, most of them looking to spend some time in one of Orlando’s amazing theme parks. Universal studios alone welcomes about 21 million visitors annually. Accompanying the large amounts of people within the parks is long lines, and long wait times. On busy days, popular rides in Orlando may never drop below an hour in wait time, and could climb up to even 3 hours or more! I’ve done my fair share of waiting in lines for popular rides, and that only made me wonder, what goes into queues and wait times within theme parks, and is there a way to know what the best time would be to enter a queue?

My investigation focused mainly on theme parks in Orlando, specifically Disneyland and Universal Studios. My final deliverable for my capstone is a forecast plot made in Jupyter Notebooks and coded in Python. This forecast plot shows predicted wait times for the month of April at Hagrid's Motorbike Adventure at Universal Studios Orlando, my favorite rollercoaster.
